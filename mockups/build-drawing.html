entropy c / physical assembly

One enclosure, one insert, one optical transform. Nothing floats. This is the proposed build—not a generated product fantasy.

ASSEMBLY STUDY · REV A

WHAT YOU BUY

  1. Container Store acrylic cube, 311 × 197 × 197 mm
  2. CoreS3, 54 × 54 × 15.5 mm
  3. CamS3, 40 × 24 × 11 mm
  4. DLight, 32 × 24 × 8 mm
  5. H-bridge, 56 × 24 × 10.2 mm
  6. Low-RPM DC gearmotor

WHAT YOU MAKE

  • One removable 3 mm insert plate
  • Mirror rotor on motor shaft
  • Frosted inlet and projection plane
  • Camera hood and small brackets
  • One notch in the removable lid for USB-C

HOW IT ASSEMBLES

  1. Fasten motor, driver, and cables below insert.
  2. Pass motor shaft through one insert hole.
  3. Mount rotor, tile, diffuser, camera, and CoreS3 above.
  4. Test that camera sees only diffuser.
  5. Place clear cube over the finished insert.

REJECTED FROM GENERATED MOCK

  • The “remote” was not a real component.
  • Nothing hangs from the enclosure roof.
  • No unsupported floating panel exists.
  • No large custom pedestal is required.
  • Five prisms are not part of Rev A.
