# NYC installation direction

The home prototype is portable. The office installation should make that prototype look intentional, protect it from casual contact, and use the 88th-floor windows at One World Trade Center as an environmental input without pointing a camera at the office or recording a recognizable view.

## Leading direction: window light instrument

Install `entropy c` on a narrow freestanding museum pedestal near—but not attached to—the window wall.

The city and sky become a changing light source:

```text
NYC daylight + clouds + weather + shadows
                    ↓
       frosted, baffled light collector
                    ↓
        moving faceted mirror assembly
                    ↓
             dichroic element
                    ↓
       abstract frosted projection plane
                    ↓
           inward-facing camera
```

The camera sees only the projection plane. It cannot focus on the skyline, people, badges, desks, or screens.

## Physical presentation

- A black or white powder-coated steel pedestal, approximately 36–42 inches tall.
- The acrylic prototype sits inside a recessed top tray.
- A clear locking museum vitrine or tamper-resistant outer cover encloses the device.
- Power and optional Ethernet run inside the pedestal and exit at the floor or wall side.
- A hidden steel plate or security cable anchors the device to the pedestal.
- The service panel is on the staff-facing rear and uses tamper-resistant screws or a keyed lock.
- The pedestal carries a short explanation and QR code; the device screen shows live health and receipts.
- No controls are exposed to visitors beyond an optional sealed momentary button.

The installation must remain freestanding unless Facilities and building management approve attachment to window mullions, walls, or floor. Nothing should be adhered directly to the glass without explicit approval.

## Using the 88th-floor windows

The windows are the distinctive NYC input, but direct sun and building rules introduce constraints.

### Desired input

- Changing daylight intensity and color temperature
- Clouds, weather, haze, sunrise, and sunset
- Fast shadows and reflections from the city environment
- Interior movement expressed only as diffuse changes in light

### Optical privacy boundary

Use a side or rear light collector made from:

1. A frosted acrylic aperture
2. A black baffle with at least one 90-degree turn
3. Optional opal diffuser film
4. The moving mirror and dichroic transform
5. A final frosted projection screen

There must be no straight optical path from the room or window to the camera. Validate this by retaining home test frames and confirming that no recognizable scene can be reconstructed.

### Sun and heat

- Do not concentrate direct sunlight with lenses or concave mirrors.
- Use UV-stable acrylic or accept the clear cover as a replaceable consumable.
- Measure internal temperature and shut down the motor/lighting above a safe threshold.
- Keep electronics shaded in the pedestal or opaque base.
- Test glare from likely viewing angles before final placement.
- Expect large seasonal changes in sun angle on the 88th floor.

## Security levels

### Home prototype

- Lift-off enclosure
- USB-C power
- Wi-Fi
- No permanent mounting

### Office demo

- Recessed pedestal tray
- Hidden security cable
- Tamper-resistant service screws
- Outbound-only Wi-Fi or approved wired networking
- Device identity key stored separately from application firmware where practical

### Long-term installation

- Locking outer vitrine
- Anchored pedestal
- Internal power/network routing
- Door-open or cover-lift tamper switch
- Signed firmware and scoped, replaceable device credentials
- Facilities, network, privacy, physical security, and security review
- Documented shutdown and removal procedure

## Visitor experience

From several feet away, a visitor should see an abstract, slowly changing projection of NYC light. Up close, the display explains three distinct signals:

- **Physical:** transformed ambient light and motion
- **Device:** hardware RNG mixed locally
- **Network:** approved aggregate traffic used as visible rhythm/context, not secret entropy

The physical installation should continue moving and showing local health when offline. Cloud connectivity adds receipts and live context; it does not make the artwork function.

## Placement studies to produce

1. **Window pedestal:** device perpendicular to glass, using a baffled side light collector.
2. **Window-facing pedestal:** collector faces glass but the camera remains fully enclosed behind the diffuser.
3. **Interior lobby pedestal:** daylight reaches the device indirectly; easier glare and thermal control.
4. **Wall niche:** only if an existing secure display niche and approved power/network path are available.

## Decisions required before an office mockup

- Photographs and dimensions of the candidate NYC location
- Distance to glass, outlets, and approved network connection
- Direct-sun exposure throughout the day
- Pedestal/vitrine standards preferred by Facilities
- Egress, cleaning, sprinkler, and accessibility clearances
- Whether the installation is supervised, lobby-facing, or badge-access-only
